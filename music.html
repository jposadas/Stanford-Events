The Beethoven Project: Stanford Philharmonia Orchestra, Stephen M. Sano, conductor, with Stanford Symphonic Chorus

Date: Friday, March 15, 2013 and Sunday, March 17, 2013

Time: Friday: 8 AM - 10 AM; Sunday: 12:30 PM - 2:30 PM

Location: Bing Concert Hall, Palo Alto, CA

Description: The Beethoven Project is a large-scale celebration acknowledging Bing Concert Hall as the new home of the Stanford Symphony Orchestra and Stanford Philharmonia Orchestra. These ensembles, under the baton of Jindong Cai, will devote the season to the performance of all nine Beethoven symphonies, as well as all five of the composer’s piano concerti featuring Van Cliburn Gold Medal–winning pianist and Stanford alumnus Jon Nakamatsu.

Philharmonia Baroque Orchestra - Program 3: The Italian Violin

Date: Wednesday, March 20, 2013

Time: 8 PM - 10 PM

Location: Bing Concert Hall, Palo Alto, CA

Description: Much of the music of Antonio Vivaldi was originally composed for the all-girl orchestra residing at the Ospedale della Pietà, the Venice orphanage that reputedly became home to many illegitimate daughters of Venetian nobility. Rachel Podger continues this tradition of exemplary performance of Vivaldi’s virtuoso violin compositions in this concert, which also features Italian Baroque string music by Corelli, Mossi, Pergolesi, and Locatelli.

Bach's St. Matthew Passion with Schola Cantorum under the Direction of Gregory Wait

Date: Friday, March 22, 2013

Time: 7 PM - 9 PM

Location: Memorial Church, Palo Alto, CA

Description: Surround yourself with the glorious music of western civilization’s supreme masterpiece, Bach’s St. Matthew Passion, which will be sung in German. This rarely performed work is presented in the sublime acoustic of Stanford Memorial Church with double chorus, double orchestra and eight soloists, including Brian Thorsett as the Evangelist and Kenneth Goodson, portraying Jesus.

Making Books Sing - Louis Armstrong: Jazz Ambassador

Date: Sunday, March 24, 2013

Time: 2:30 PM - 4:30 PM

Location: Bing Concert Hall, Palo Alto, CA

Description: Making Books Sing musical theater company returns with their new work celebrating the life and music of Louis Armstrong. Back in the fifties, when the United States had a diplomatic mission of utmost importance, it deployed its supersonic weapon, Louis "Satchmo" Armstrong. Known worldwide for his toothy grin, fun-loving personality and the brass brilliance of his jazz cornet, nobody spread the gospel of goodwill across the globe quite like Ambassador Satch. But when nine children from Little Rock, Arkansas are denied admission to school, Louis is forced to ask: is American equality really for all?

Date: Friday, March 29, 2013

Choir of New College, Oxford

Date: Wednesday, April 3, 2013

Time: 8 PM - 10 PM

Location: Memorial Church, Palo Alto, CA

Dating back to the 14th century, the Choir of New College, Oxford is one of the UK’s most celebrated choral groups. Based at Oxford University and renowned worldwide through its recordings and tours, the 40-member choir comprises young men plus boys (ages 7–13) who study music intensively at the New College School. For this rare visit to the Bay Area, the Choir will collaborate with the Stanford Chamber Chorale in a program that celebrates the centenary of Benjamin Britten.
